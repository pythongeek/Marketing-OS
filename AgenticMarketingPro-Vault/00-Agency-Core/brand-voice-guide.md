# Brand Voice Guide

## The 5 voice principles

### 1. Numbers up front, never adjectives first
- ✅ "We grew organic traffic 47% in Q1 by rebuilding the topic cluster around high-intent commercial keywords."
- ❌ "We achieved incredible, game-changing, transformative growth through our innovative approach."

### 2. Define the term, then use it
- ✅ "AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — the practice of getting cited by AI search engines like Perplexity and ChatGPT Search — is now 12% of our clients' referral traffic."
- ❌ "AEO is critical for modern visibility." (assumes reader knows the term)

### 3. Acknowledge the trade-off
- ✅ "Programmatic SEO at scale trades content depth for breadth. We mitigate this with [specific guardrail]."
- ❌ "Programmatic SEO is a powerful growth lever." (no trade-off acknowledged)

### 4. Cite the source
- ✅ "According to Ahrefs' 2025 study of 100K SERPs, the #1 result averages 1,447 words."
- ❌ "Long-form content ranks better." (uncited)

### 5. End with what to do next
- ✅ "If you're a Series A SaaS company, audit your top 20 commercial keywords for AEO citation in the next 7 days."
- ❌ "The future of search is here." (no action)

## Tone calibration
| Situation | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Client reporting (good news) | Confident, factual | "MRR from organic grew 32% QoQ, driven by 4 pillar articles published in Q4." |
| Client reporting (bad news) | Direct, no spin | "Organic traffic declined 12% this month. Root cause: Google March core update. Recovery plan attached." |
| Thought leadership | Sharp, slightly contrarian | "Stop building content calendars. They're optimized for output, not outcomes." |
| Sales/pitch | Evidence-led, no hype | "We ran the same audit on your top 3 competitors. Findings attached. If we work together, here's what the first 90 days look like." |
| Crisis communication | Calm, decisive | "Here's what happened. Here's what we're doing. Here's what to expect next." |

## Forbidden phrases (auto-rejected by QA Agent)
- "Game-changing", "revolutionary", "next-generation", "cutting-edge", "leverage" (as a verb), "synergy", "best-in-class", "world-class", "seamless", "robust"
- Any emoji in client-facing content (except in social media posts where platform-native)
- "AI-powered" without a specific capability described in the next sentence
- "We believe" or "we think" — replace with what we *do* or what the *data shows*

## Preferred vocabulary
| Use this | Not this |
|---|---|
| Compound, compounding | Scale (overused) |
| Outcome | Result (vague) |
| Specific | Granular (jargon) |
| Trade-off | Compromise (negative connotation) |
| Evidence | Proof (absolute) |
| Visibility | Awareness (marketing jargon) |

## Sample paragraph (reference for QA Agent)
> We tracked Acme's brand mentions across 8 AI engines weekly for 90 days. ChatGPT cited them in 14% of tracked queries at baseline. After deploying entity-rich schema across 47 pages and publishing 3 corroborating case studies on third-party sites, citation rate rose to 31% — a 121% lift. The lift correlated with a 23% increase in branded organic search, suggesting AI citations drive downstream search behavior. We're now replicating the playbook across 4 other clients in the same vertical.
